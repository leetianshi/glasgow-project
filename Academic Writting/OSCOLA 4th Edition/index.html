    OSCOLA 4th Edition

    1. Frequently Referenced Items

    1.1 Parts of Statutes

    Full Form Abbreviation
    part/parts pt/pts
    section/sections s/ss
    subsection/subsections sub-s/sub-ss
    paragraph/paragraphs para/paras
    subparagraph/subparagraphs subpara/subparas
    schedule/schedules sch/schs

    Example 1: Consumer Protection Act 1987, s 2.
    Example 2: Human Rights Act 1998, s 15(1)(b).
    Example 3: Criminal Attempts Act 1981, ss 1(1) and 4(3).
    Example 4: Sexual Offences Act 2003, s 1(1)(c).

    1.2 EU Cases

    case number| case name| EU:C case number| report number| [exact paragraph].

    Example 1: Case C-176/03 Commission v Council EU:C:2005:542, [2005] ECR I-7879.
    Example 2: Case C-542/09 Commission v the Netherlands EU:C:2012:346.

    1.3 Books

    1.3.1 Authored Books

    author,| title| (additional information,| edition,| publisher| year)| exact page.

    Example 1: Timothy Endicott, Administrative Law (OUP 2009).
    Example 2: Gareth Jones, Goff and Jones: The Law of Restitution (1st supp, 7th edn, Sweet & Maxwell 2009).

    1.3.2 Contributions to Edited Books

    author,| title| in editor (ed),| book title| (additional information,| publisher| year)| exact page.

    Example 1: Justine Pila, The Value of Authorship in the Digital Environment in William H Dutton and Paul W Jeffreys (eds), World Wide Research: Reshaping the Sciences and Humanities in the Century of Information (MIT Press 2010).
    Example 2: John Cartwright, The Fiction of the Reasonable Man in AG Castermans and others (eds), Ex Libris Hans Nieuwenhuis (Kluwer 2009).

    1.3.3 Abbreviations

    Full Form Abbreviation
    part/parts pt/pts
    chapters/chapters ch/chs
    paragraph/paragraphs para/paras
    page/pages stand alone, without p/pp

    1.4 Journal Articles

    author,| title| (year)| volume| journal name or abbreviation| first page of article,| exact page.
    [OR]
    author,| title| [year]| journal name or abbreviation| first page of article,| exact page.

    Example 1: Paul Craig, Theory, Pure Theory and Values in Public Law [2005] PL 440.
    Example 2: Alison L Young, In Defence of Due Deference (2009) 72 MLR 554.

    1.5 Working Papers

    author,| title| (year)| publisher| additional information,| exact page| <link>| accessed date.

    Example: John M Finnis, On Public Reason (2006) Oxford Legal Studies Research Paper 1/2007, 8 <http://ssrn.com/abstract=955815> accessed 18 November 2009.

    1.6 European Commission Documents

    Commission,| title| COM number,| exact page.

    Example 1: Commission, Proposal for a Council Decision on the conclusion, on behalf of the European Community, of the Protocol on the Implementation of the Alpine Convention in the Field of Transport (Transport Protocol) COM (2008) 895 final, ch I, art 3.
    Example 2: Commission, Action Plan on consumer access to justice and the settlement of disputes in the internal market (Communication) COM (96) 13 final.
    Example 3: Commission, Proposal for a Council Regulation on jurisdiction and the recognition and enforcement of judgments in civil and commercial matters COM (99) 348 final.

    1.7 Conference Papers

    author,| titile| (title of the conference,| location of the conference,| date of the conference)| exact page.

    Example: Ben McFarlane and Donal Nolan, Remedying Reliance: The Future Development of Promissory and Proprietary Estoppel in English Law (Obligations III conference, Brisbane, July 2006).

    1.8 Websites

    author,| title| (publisher,| date)| <link>| accessed date.

    Example: Sarah Cole, Virtual Friend Fires Employee (Naked Law, 1 May 2009) <www.nakedlaw.com/2009/05/index.html> accessed 19 November 2009.

    1.9 General Principle (e.g. Report)

    author,| title| (additional information,| publisher| year)| exact page.
